ABOUT THIS RECOMMENDATION
The 180° shutter rule says your shutter speed should be roughly 2× your frame rate for natural motion. At your chosen frame rate, the ideal shutter is too slow for bright daylight without an ND filter — the image would blow out. This filter brings the exposure back in line.

If you notice your footage has a slight color cast with this filter, use a +/-5 temperature correction in your editor. Budget filters sometimes skew magenta or yellow.

WHY THIS MATTERS
When shutter speed is too fast relative to frame rate, motion freezes frame-by-frame and footage looks stuttery or "video-y." When it's too slow, everything smears. The 180° rule (shutter ≈ 2× frame rate) matches the natural motion blur your eye expects.

On a bright day, hitting the ideal shutter without an ND filter is usually impossible — the camera would blow out the image. That's why NDs exist. Pair this calculator with the ND Filter Recommender.

HOW TO USE THIS
Azimuth tells you where on the compass the sun/moon is. 0° = North, 90° = East, 180° = South, 270° = West.

Altitude tells you how high above the horizon. 0° = on the horizon, 90° = straight overhead.

Golden hour is when the sun is between 0° and ~6° altitude. Warm tones, long shadows, flattering light for everything you'd film with a drone.

Position is calculated from your device's GPS + time. Works offline once location is set.
